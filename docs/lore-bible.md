# Lore Bible — Mzansi Underworld

Canonical. Code, prompts, copy must align. Disagree? Open an ADR.

## Setting

A composite SA metropolis. **Not real-named** Joburg/Cape Town/Durban — we draw from each but the in-world city is fictional, so we avoid defaming real neighborhoods.

Working name for in-world capital: **eGoli** (informal Zulu name for Joburg, "place of gold").

Provinces in fiction match real SA. Areas inspired by:

- **Hillbrow / Yeoville analogues** — high-density flats, immigrant communities, nightlife, crime.
- **Sandton analogue** — corporate towers, new money, security estates.
- **Soweto analogues** — historic townships, family compounds, taxi ranks, shisa nyamas.
- **Cape coast analogue** — tourism, port, smuggling, mountain townships inland.
- **Durban harbor analogue** — port, smuggling, Indian/Zulu blended culture.

## Tone

Tsotsi. Gomora. Yizo Yizo. The Queen. Blood & Water.

- Crime is **grounded** — no Hollywood pyrotechnics.
- Cops are **human** — corrupt and brave in the same week.
- Families are **anchors** — even gang bosses answer to gogo (grandmother).
- Humor is **dry** and earned, never the meme variety.

## Factions (canon)

### Criminal
- **Top Six** — taxi association turned syndicate. Controls minibus economy, plus protection rackets along routes.
- **Iron Hand** — ex-security-company breakaway. CIT robberies, firearms, ex-SAPS members.
- **Khanyisa** — cell-structured drug network, sources from KZN port.
- **Independent crews** — fluid, recruited from townships, often absorbed or extinguished.

### Police / Government
- **Provincial Police Service (PPS)** — uniformed, frontline. Mixed quality, vulnerable to corruption.
- **Special Crimes Unit (SCU)** — Hawks analogue. Investigates organized crime.
- **Internal Affairs (IA)** — investigates own.
- **Provincial Government** — tender contracts, BEE quotas, factionalism.

### Business
- Taxi associations (criminal-adjacent).
- Shisa nyamas / taverns (neighborhood institutions, info brokers).
- Security companies (legal cover for ex-criminals; armed).
- Logistics / construction (laundering candidates).
- Mining suppliers (Phase 2+).

### Families
Family names should sample broadly across SA ethnic groups. The lore strictly avoids assigning ethnic identity to criminality.

## Music / culture references

- Amapiano, gqom, kwaito, maskandi — referenced by genre. No copyrighted track titles in generated radio content; use original synthesized cues.

## Hard "Do Not" list

- No real public figures.
- No real political party names.
- No real police unit names beyond generic ranks.
- No real journalist names.
- No real victim names from real crimes.
- No xenophobic tropes.
- No "the X community are criminals" tropes — criminality is structural and personal, not ethnic.
